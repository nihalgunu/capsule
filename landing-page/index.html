The Problem

Complex systems are impossible to reason about

What happens if we raise interest rates? If a supplier fails? If we change this policy? Traditional models are brittle—they capture first-order effects but miss the cascades. The real world is interconnected. Every action has consequences that ripple outward in ways that spreadsheets can't predict.

We needed a new approach: systems that model causal chains, trace ripple effects, and explain every consequence.

The Breakthrough

Gemini's 1M token context changes everything

With a million tokens of context, an entire system state fits in memory. Every variable. Every relationship. Every historical precedent. The model doesn't just respond—it reasons about complex systems where past actions shape present reality.

Supply chains adapt to disruption. Markets respond to intervention. Resources flow and deplete. Consequences cascade with full traceability.

Chronicle

Our demo: rewrite 6,000 years of history

To prove the engine works, we built Chronicle—an alternate history game. Zoom into any point on a 3D globe. Make a change—"Rome never falls," "The printing press is invented in China"—and watch consequences ripple across continents and millennia.

History is familiar terrain. Everyone intuitively understands cause and effect across time. It makes the engine's power tangible. If it can model civilization, it can model your supply chain.
